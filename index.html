Ingredients

  • 8 eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp black soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp black tea leaves
  • 2 pods star anise
  • 1 (2-inch) piece cinnamon stick
  • 1 tbsp tangerine zest

Tea Leaf Eggs

Description

Fragrant and flavorful tea eggs are a traditional Chinese dish. The original recipe uses various spices, soy sauce, and black tea leaves.

Directions

  1. In a large saucepan, combine eggs and 1 teaspoon salt; cover with cold water.
  2. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, drain, and cool. When cool, tap eggs with the back of a spoon to crack shells (do not remove shells).
  4. In a large saucepan, combine 3 cups water, soy sauce, black soy sauce, salt, tea leaves, star anise, cinnamon stick, and tangerine zest.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 3 hours.
  6. Remove from heat, add eggs, and let steep for at least 8 hours. Note: Eggs can steep up to 1 1/2 days for richer flavor. Store eggs unpeeled and tightly sealed in refrigerator. They will keep 4 to 5 days.
  7. Enjoy!
